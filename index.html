Ginkgo Health APP

2022 - Present

I joined CEO Maggie Deng at Ginkgo Health to build a gamified healthy ageing app for seniors.

As the solo designer on the team, I worked closely with talented people at Ginkgo Health and developers at TTT Studios to innovate, design, and launch our first app.

Nov ‘22 Ginkgo Testing Event, Vancouver BC

Ginkgo’s core feature is an expert AI system that adapts to your unique lifestyle and health conditions, helping you prevent injuries and chronic conditions through daily, home-based exercise routines.

Personalizing exercise reduces the risks of injury and over-activity, which is important to seniors.

1ST CHALLENGE: Transforming a complex health assessment into easily digestible experiences for a frictionless onboarding process.

After designing the first version of our product and talking to many early users, we noticed a recurring problem:

People want a personalized experience they can trust, but felt insecure about sharing personal information in the assessment process.

So, working with Rob, Ginkgo's lead kinesiologist, we improved the onboarding experience by transforming the assessment from a form into a conversation, which feels more personal and less intimidating.

By creating a persona from trustable health experts that our users interact with regularly, we were able to design a trustable character, Professor Ginkgo, to represent our expert AI system.

This was incredibly successful as we found that seniors were more willing to open up when they are speaking to someone they see as their personal health expert.

Seniors could share with Professor Ginkgo as much as they felt comfortable with, and Professor Ginkgo generates individualized prescriptions they can trust.

2ND CHALLENGE: Improving user retention and motivation with new gamification features.

In the next session of testing, we learned that many people who were new to exercising were not motivated to log in to exercise every day. They expressed common pain points, most notably:

  • People need help visualizing the physical benefits of daily exercise but their training won’t produce noticeable results without weeks of perseverance.
  • People are busy! Schedules change every week and they often miss training days as a result.

Nov ‘22 Ginkgo Testing Event, Vancouver BC

We found through studies with our kinesiology partners that the most reliable way of encouraging users to exercise every day is through intrinsic motivation, self-actualization, and behaviour modification:

Autonomy: setting your own weekly training goals, committing to healthy habit challenges

Using the official WHO exercise guidelines to direct users in setting their own weekly goals help create trust in their prescription and build intrinsic motivation to complete scheduled trainings.

Their prescription is also fully customizable, allowing users to freely swap, add, and remove their training days without compromising their progress.

3RD CHALLENGE: Ensure the app is safe and accessible to the target senior population.

We found that seniors were keen to learn new, healthy behaviours and understand the benefits of tech in such task, but they faced many physical and mental barriers that deter them from adopting a new fitness app.

  • We chose the tablet over smartphones because the larger screen allows for detailed exercise animations, bigger font styles, and bigger buttons.
  • Visual captions and audio narrations throughout the assessment and training makes the experience accessible for all.
  • Daily training sessions adjust the level of difficulty based on the user's current conditions to avoid injury or strain on their body.
  • During training, "too hard/too easy” buttons teach our expert AI system which exercises are ineffective or too difficult so it can learn and adapt.

With the launch of our first version, we were able to share our story to many communities in BC, Canada, showing seniors how easy it can be to exercise and rebuild confidence in their physical body.

It was extremely motivating designing new features for Ginkgo Health and watching testers really enjoy the experience. I got to work with amazing and passionate people like Maggie Deng, Robert Kjemhus, and Vidhi Shah. We all believed in Ginkgo’s potential impact on each of our lives which pushed us to go the extra mile.

Dec ‘23 Team Building, Vancouver BC

This experience has taught me many valuable skills in leading the design process, designing complex systems and user flows, and managing product development. I also learned first hand the importance of communication, iteration, and focus when building a new product from scratch.
