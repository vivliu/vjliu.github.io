I joined CEO Maggie Deng at Ginkgo Health to build a gamified healthy ageing app for seniors. For the majority of the project I was the solo designer on the team, working closely with many talented people at Ginkgo Health and developers at TTT Studios to innovate, design, and launch the Ginkgo Health app.

Ginkgo‚Äôs core feature is an expert AI system that adapts to your unique lifestyle and health conditions, helping you prevent injuries and chronic conditions through daily, home-based exercise routines. Personalized exercise prescriptions reduce the risks of injury and over-activity for optimal results, which is very important to seniors.

After designing the first version of our product and talking to many early users, we noticed a recurring problem. People felt insecure about sharing personal information in the assessment process and didn‚Äôt get much in return because their training won‚Äôt produce noticeable effects without weeks or months of perseverance.

So, working with Rob, Ginkgo's lead kinesiologist and health expert, we revamped Ginkgo Health with two new concepts centered around the user:

  • Build trust between the user and our expert AI system
  • Nurture intrinsic motivation to exercise with a gamified behavioural support system

Dec ‚Äò23 Team Building, Vancouver BC

The new assessment is structured like a conversation with our AI, Prof. Ginkgo, and we found that seniors were more willing to open up when they speak to a health expert. We created the character, Prof. Ginkgo, from personas of personal trainers that our users have worked with. Seniors could share with Prof. Ginkgo as much as they felt comfortable with, and Prof. Ginkgo generates individualized prescriptions they can trust.

Then we started work on new gamification features, the foundation of our user retention strategy.

We wanted Ginkgo to accompany seniors their whole life, and to do so we needed to nurture a healthy intrinsic motivation to exercise every day. After analyzing testing feedback, we prioritized the following features:

  • Autonomy:¬†setting your own weekly training goals, committing to healthy habit challenges
  • Competence: weekly activity feedback specific to their daily evercises, monthly performance measures to track long term progress
  • Relatedness:¬†sharing activity badges and performance trophies with family and friends, send gifts to celebrate each others achievements

There is one design rule that we maintained throughout this revamped experience: Ginkgo must always be safe and accessible to the target senior population.

This meant UI had clear labels and functions were easy to understand. Daily training sessions adjusted the level of difficulty based on how you currently feel to avoid injury or strain. During training, "too hard/too easy‚Äù buttons let you teach the AI¬†system what exercises you are having trouble with so it can learn and adapt.

Using the official WHO weekly exercise guidelines to help users set their own weekly workout goals help create trust in our prescriptions and build intrinsic motivation to complete their scheduled training.

We also built a virtual world based on tropical island resorts with fun island themes to unlock and locations to explore, all designed to gamify the exercise experience and motivate seniors to log on every day.

  • Knowledge library: a place to learn, debunk myths, and share knowledge articles with friends and family
  • Ginkgo Centre: visit Professor Ginkgo in his office to update your assessment every month

Our mission is to build Ginkgo Health into a holistic digital community centre for healthy ageing, to lower the barriers so anyone can start exercising. With the launch of our first version, we were able to share our story to many communities in BC, Canada, and show seniors how easy it can be to exercise - to rebuild confidence in their physical body.
